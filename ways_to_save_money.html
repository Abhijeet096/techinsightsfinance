10 Smart Ways to Save Money as a Student

1. Track Your Expenses

Use a notebook or apps like Money Lover to record every rupee you spend. Knowing where your money goes helps you cut unnecessary expenses.

Pro Tip: Review your spending weekly to spot patterns and save more!

2. Cook Your Own Meals

Eating out or ordering food adds up fast. Learn simple recipes like Maggi, khichdi, or sandwiches to save money and eat healthier.

Pro Tip: Buy groceries in bulk with friends to split costs!

3. Use Student Discounts

Many brands offer student discounts on apps, clothes, and subscriptions. Use your college ID or platforms like UNiDAYS to unlock deals.

Pro Tip: Check for discounts on software like Adobe or Spotify for students!

4. Buy Second-Hand Books

Textbooks are expensive! Look for used books on Amazon, OLX, or local bookstores. You can also borrow from seniors or libraries.

Pro Tip: Sell your old books after the semester to recover some cash!

5. Share Subscriptions

Split the cost of Netflix, YouTube Premium, or other subscriptions with friends or roommates to save big.

Pro Tip: Always check the platformâ€™s terms to ensure sharing is allowed!

6. Use Public Transport

Avoid cabs and use buses, metro, or shared autos. Many cities offer student passes for cheaper travel.

Pro Tip: Walk or cycle for short distances to save even more!

7. Avoid Impulse Buying

Before buying anything, wait 24 hours to decide if you really need it. This helps avoid wasting money on unnecessary items.

Pro Tip: Make a shopping list and stick to it!

8. Earn Extra Income

Take up part-time gigs like tutoring, freelancing, or content creation to boost your pocket money. Check out my tutoring guide for ideas!

Pro Tip: Use platforms like Upwork or Preply to find quick gigs!

9. Save on Stationery

Buy stationery in bulk during sales or use digital tools like Notion or Google Keep for note-taking to cut costs.

Pro Tip: Reuse old notebooks for rough work!

10. Set Savings Goals

Save a small amount each month for big goals like a new phone or a trip. Use a piggy bank or a separate savings account.

Pro Tip: Automate small transfers to your savings account to make it effortless!
